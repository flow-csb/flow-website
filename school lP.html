University Food Waste Reduction Programs

Food waste is a significant challenge for institutions like universities, but it also presents valuable opportunities for cost savings, environmental responsibility, and educational experiences. Here's how our program works, the benefits it can deliver, and areas for potential collaboration.

How it Works

Collection

Food waste is collected from dining halls, events, and other sources, then sorted and transported to the next stage.

Management

Collected waste is managed through composting, anaerobic digestion, or other methods to break down organic matter.

Disposal & Repurposing

Processed waste is disposed of in an environmentally friendly manner or repurposed for uses like animal feed or soil amendment.

Benefits and Opportunities

Cost Savings

Reducing waste can lower disposal costs and generate savings through repurposing for energy, animal feed, or other uses.

Environmental Responsibility

Waste reduction supports sustainability goals by minimizing landfill contributions and harnessing food waste for positive purposes.

Educational Experiences

Students, faculty, and staff can learn about waste management, sustainability, and other topics through hands-on involvement with waste reduction programs.

Collaboration Areas

We are open to collaboration in various areas such as feedstock supplies, renewable power uptake, and more. If you are interested in partnering with us, please reach out to our team.
